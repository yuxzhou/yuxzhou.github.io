Hi! I’m a **Schmidt AI in Science Fellow** at the University of Oxford, working within the groups of Prof. Volker L. Deringer and Prof. Jakob Foerster, and advised by Prof. Stephen R. Elliott. My research sits at the frontier of "AI for Science," focusing on the intersection of:

* **Machine learning potentials (MLPs) for atomistic modeling** – Developing hierarchical training strategies and high-efficiency potentials (GAP/ACE) to bridge the gap between quantum accuracy and device-scale engineering. I build models capable of simulating realistic devices (over 500,000 atoms) to capture complex physical processes like the full "write-erase" cycle of memory chips.
* **Computational study of amorphous functional materials** – Uncovering the atomic-scale mechanisms of phase transitions and transport in disordered materials. My work combines chemical bonding analysis with atomistic simulations to solve critical industry bottlenecks, such as designing ultrafast Sc-Sb-Te alloys for storage and developing phase-change heterostructures to suppress resistance drift.
* **Generative modelling of amorphous materials** – Leveraging generative AI to explore the vast, complex configurational spaces of disordered solids (such as amorphous oxides for TOPCon solar cells). This work aims to accelerate the discovery of structure-property relationships and generate physically meaningful structures at speeds orders of magnitude faster than traditional Molecular Dynamics.

My research has led to 12 first/co-first author publications in leading venues including **Science (x2)**, **Nature Electronics**, **Nature Communications**, **Advanced Materials**, and **Angewandte Chemie**. My work on device-scale modeling was highlighted in a *Nature Electronics* Research Briefing as a paradigm shift for electronic design. I have also been recognized with the **E\PCOS Best Oral Presentation Award** and hold authorized patents for material designs that directly address challenges in the semiconductor industry.

I am passionate about bridging the gap between theoretical material physics and practical device application, aiming to build the next generation of "atomistic TCAD" tools. I actively look for opportunities to connect atomic-scale insights with macroscopic device performance.

I’m always excited to collaborate with talented people across the fields of AI, materials science, and semiconductor engineering. Whether it’s discussing new ML architectures for materials or exploring industrial applications, **let’s discuss!**
